## Chain 1: Hawkish Fed Repricing Crushes Risk, Hits Metals and Tech

**The driver:** Bond traders repricing for a more hawkish Fed path — corroborated by Deutsche Bank cutting gold forecasts by up to 22% and headlines flagging renewed rate-hike fears — drove a broad risk-off session, with the US yield curve bear-steepening and tech leading equities lower.

**The chain:**

**Rates reprice higher across the curve:** Going into today, the prior session saw US yields rise sharply across the belly and long end — the 6M added +6.0 bps to 3.9800%, the 2Y rose +5.0 bps to 4.2400%, the 10Y climbed +5.0 bps to 4.5100%, and the 30Y pushed up +5.0 bps to 4.9500%. The very short end (SOFR at 3.6100%, -1.0 bps; 1M at 3.6600%, -3.0 bps) held steady or fell, creating a classic bear-steepening pattern: the market is not pricing in imminent cuts, but is adding a premium further out for stubborn inflation and a Fed that has now paused repeatedly without pivoting dovish. Bond traders, per Bloomberg, are waiting on personal spending data to confirm whether this hawkish repositioning is warranted.

**Gold and silver capitulate:** Rate-hike fears mechanically raise the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets. Gold fell -1.26% to 4129.0000 and silver collapsed -5.95% to 61.6300 — a much steeper decline in silver, which carries higher beta to risk sentiment. Deutsche Bank cutting its gold forecasts by up to 22% is both a symptom and an accelerant: once a major bank publishes a forecast cut of that magnitude, leveraged longs that had accumulated on prior geopolitical fear must unwind. Platinum (-1.04% to 1652.9000) and palladium (-1.48% to 1238.5000) confirm the move is broad across the precious metals complex, not idiosyncratic to gold.

**Industrial metals sold in sympathy — but a tension exists:** Copper fell -3.64% to 6.1250, aluminium -3.81% to 4.3310, zinc -3.26% to 11.9400, and nickel -3.83% to 274.6545 — a synchronised, deep selloff in base metals that typically signals fears of demand destruction rather than supply disruption. The tension here is that copper also had a tailwind: Bloomberg reported prices rose on Iran peace-talk progress, as Strait of Hormuz recovery would ease global inflation. Yet copper is still down sharply on the day, which tells you the hawkish-Fed / demand-destruction channel overwhelmed any geopolitical relief bid. The rates repricing is the dominant force.

**Equities reprice duration and risk:** Higher long-end yields compress the present value of future earnings most acutely for long-duration assets. The Nasdaq 100 fell -3.29% to 29347.2734, a significantly larger decline than the Dow Jones (-0.09% to 51666.8398), which is heavy with value and dividend names. The S&P 500 dropped -1.44% to 7365.4600 and the Russell 2000 -0.96% to 2975.4810. This is textbook duration-differentiated selling: growth/tech craters, value/industrials survive. The VIX spiked +12.79% to 19.4900, approaching the "two-decade high" level flagged in the MarketWatch tech-fear-gauge article — a signal that options markets are pricing elevated tail risk specifically in tech, not just broad equities.

**The euro and commodity currencies bear the FX brunt:** A stronger dollar (DXY +0.34% to 101.3650) is the natural corollary of US rates moving higher relative to peers. EUR/USD fell -0.66% to 1.1387, pressured further by Lagarde explicitly signalling no need for a more forceful ECB response — a dovish signal that widens the rate differential versus the US. The AUD/USD selloff of -1.20% to 0.6919 and NZD/USD -0.99% to 0.5678 are consistent with both USD strength and the industrial metals collapse (both currencies carry high sensitivity to Chinese commodity demand). USD/SEK surged +1.48% to 9.7304 and USD/MXN +1.15% to 17.5472, confirming the move is a broad risk-off dollar bid rather than a US-specific story.

**Gilts diverge sharply — a separate UK dynamic:** UK gilts moved dramatically against the global trend: the 5Y Gilt added +9.8 bps to 4.3559%, the 10Y +9.8 bps to 4.8984%, and the 20Y +9.9 bps to 5.5808%. This dwarfs US yield moves and is not explained by the Fed narrative alone. BOE Governor Bailey simultaneously defended holding rates as "the sensible decision" while acknowledging energy prices remain above pre-war levels and infrastructure damage in the Middle East remains unassessed — a stagflationary framing that markets are interpreting as rates-higher-for-longer. GBP/USD was nearly flat at -0.02% to 1.3205 despite the broad dollar rally, which is consistent with gilt yields being a credibility/inflation-premium story rather than a growth-positive rate story. EUR/GBP fell -0.69% to 0.8619, reflecting the UK's relatively higher rate path versus the ECB.

**What to watch:** Monitor this Thursday's US personal consumption expenditure (PCE) data — if core PCE prints above consensus, the 2Y yield (currently 4.2400%) will test 4.40%+, validating the hawkish repricing and adding another leg down to the Nasdaq 100. Watch whether gold holds the 4100 level on a closing basis; a break below would confirm institutional de-grossing rather than mere profit-taking, and Deutsche Bank's forecast cuts would become self-fulfilling. Track EUR/USD at the 1.1350 level: a sustained break lower would signal the ECB/Fed divergence trade is re-engaging in earnest, which would add pressure to European equities already down -0.98% to -1.28% across the board.
