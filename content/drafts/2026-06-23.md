**The driver:** The Federal Reserve's hawkish reassessment — Bank of America projecting a series of rate hikes after the Fed "loses patience" with post-tariff supply-shock inflation — is repricing the US rates curve and triggering a broad risk-off unwind, with the US 10Y rising to 4.4930% (+4.2 bps) and the VIX surging +12.79% to 19.4900.

**The chain:**

**Fed repricing forces the front end higher:** Bond traders are repositioning for a higher-for-longer, possibly higher-still Fed path. The US 6M yield rose +6.0 bps to 3.9800%, the US 2Y rose +5.0 bps to 4.2400%, and the US 10Y climbed +4.2 bps to 4.4930%. The move is parallel and broad-based across the curve, which rules out a technical squeeze and confirms a genuine repricing of the Fed terminal rate expectation. The upcoming PCE personal spending data — cited by bond traders as the week's key test — is the near-term arbiter of whether this repricing holds.

**Higher US real rates crush gold and silver:** When the forward path of nominal rates rises and geopolitical fear premium fades simultaneously — here from Iran peace-talk "major progress" and the Treasury's 60-day Iranian oil sales license — the two pillars supporting gold collapse together. Gold fell -1.29% to 4128.0000 and silver cratered -12.91% to 61.5700. Deutsche Bank cutting gold forecasts by up to 22% amplifies the sentiment shift, signaling that institutional demand is re-rating lower, not just short-term positioning.

**Risk-off and rate fears hammer tech equities; Nasdaq leads the damage:** Higher discount rates compress long-duration equity valuations mechanically — Nvidia's chip-pricing concerns (Kalshi traders betting prices are falling) layer on a sector-specific earnings-risk narrative. The Nasdaq 100 fell -3.29% to 29347.2734 while the Dow Jones dropped only -0.09% to 51666.8398, a classic duration-and-growth underperformance signature. The VIX at 19.4900, up +12.79%, confirms options markets are buying tail protection against further tech-driven drawdown, consistent with the MarketWatch warning that the tech "fear gauge" is nearing a two-decade high.

**USD strengthens as rate-differential gap widens against Europe:** Higher US yields with a hawkish Fed contrasts sharply with Lagarde's ECB, which sees no need for a more forceful response to Middle East inflation fallout and expects a return to target. The EZ 10Y actually fell to 3.0094% (-2.7 bps) while the US 10Y rose, widening the rate differential. EUR/USD fell -0.68% to 1.1384 and the DXY rose +0.37% to 101.3930. Commodity currencies took the heaviest FX hit: AUD/USD -1.21% to 0.6918 and NZD/USD -1.15% to 0.5669, reflecting the combined pressure of USD strength and collapsing industrial metals prices.

**Industrial metals collapse as the inflation-and-growth double narrative reverses:** Copper had rallied earlier on Iran peace-talk progress — the thesis being that Hormuz normalization would ease supply-chain inflation. That optimism is now being overwhelmed by the Fed-hike narrative: tighter financial conditions imply weaker global demand, and a stronger dollar makes dollar-denominated metals more expensive for foreign buyers. Copper fell -3.56% to 6.1305, aluminium -3.81% to 4.3310, nickel -3.83% to 274.6545, and zinc -3.26% to 11.9400 — a synchronized decline that rules out idiosyncratic supply factors and points squarely to a macro demand-and-dollar repricing.

BOE Governor Bailey calling holding rates a "sensible decision" while citing persistent above-pre-war energy prices and unresolved Middle East infrastructure damage suggests the UK faces a stagflationary squeeze distinct from the US narrative. GBP/USD held relatively firm at -0.04% to 1.3202, meaning the Gilt selloff is not sterling-positive — rather, it reflects a UK-specific fiscal/inflation credibility concern running parallel to but separate from the global Fed-repricing driver.
